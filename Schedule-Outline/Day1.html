Day 1: Single Variable

Introduction

Objectives

  1. Associate modeling settings with calculus topics
  2. Evaluate calculus topics in terms of how they contribute to modeling
  3. Create and share materials for teaching calculus with modeling
  4. Learn a computational infrastructure to support modeling

Associate model settings with calculus topics

We'll follow a somewhat conventional sequence – 1 variable, multiple variables, differential equations – even though this may not be an appropriate way to organize a modeling-based course. For instance, in Macalester's Math 135, functions of multiple variables are introduced from the very beginning and partial derivatives are introduced from the start of differentiation.

We're doing this in order to facilitate modeling topics being used in conventional courses. We don't want to impose on you a requirement that you do the first calc as a multivariable course.

Evaluate calculus topics

The conventional set of topics doesn't always support modeling well. Insofar as a main goal of teaching calculus is to teach modeling, some new topics need to be introduced (e.g. parameters, fitting). Some calculus topics (e.g. limits, algebraic rules for integration) have very little to do with modeling

Many of the techniques needed for modeling, e.g. parameter estimation, require computation. At the same time, having an appropriate computational infrastructure allows calculus to be taught in new ways.
